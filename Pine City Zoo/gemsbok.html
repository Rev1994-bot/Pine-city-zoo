Gemsboks are social animals and are often found in small herds. One of the most remarkable aspects of gemsbok biology is their ability to survive without drinking water, obtaining moisture from the plants they eat and conserving water efficiently.

Gemsboks are listed as Least Concern on the IUCN Red List, and their populations are considered stable in many areas. However, they still face threats from habitat degradation and hunting. In some regions, gemsboks have been introduced as part of conservation efforts or for hunting purposes.
